Recycled Plastic Raincoat

Project 1: Making a Raincoat from Recycled Plastic Bags

In Project 1, the focus is on creating a raincoat by recycling plastic bags, showcasing a sustainable approach to waste management. The process begins with collecting used plastic bags, followed by sorting and cleaning them to ensure they are free from contaminants. The cleaned bags are then joined together using heat (iron). The next step involves designing the raincoat, where considerations such as size, functionality, and aesthetics are addressed. The final product is then tested for durability and water resistance, ensuring it meets practical needs.

This project tackles several important issues:

  • Plastic Pollution: By recycling plastic, we reduce the amount of waste that ends up in landfills and the environment.
  • Saves Marine Lives: Reducing plastic pollution helps protect marine life from harmful plastic waste.
  • Making Something Useful: We turn discarded plastic into functional items like raincoats, giving new life to waste materials.
  • Endless Possibilities: This method can be used to create a wide range of products, demonstrating the versatility of recycled plastic.
  • Recycling Plastic: It promotes the recycling of plastic, encouraging a more sustainable approach to managing plastic waste.

    • This project shows how STEM can be applied to solve pressing environmental problems and create valuable products from waste materials.

  • S (Science): We study the properties of plastic and how it can be transformed into new materials. This includes understanding the chemical processes involved in recycling plastic and how to treat it so it can be used to make items like raincoats.
  • T (Technology): Technology plays a key role in processing and molding the recycled plastic. We use tools to melt, the plastic into new forms. Technology also helps in designing the processes for creating durable and functional products.
  • M (Mathematics): Mathematics helps in determining the right ratios and measurements for processing the plastic.
